## Intentions of Technology Matter

GenAI is built upon the idea that laws only apply to the poor, the world is doomed anyway so we should exploit it, and people and their labor is only valuable in relation to its short term economic impact. It doesn't matter how you use the technology personally, the way this tech was built and implemented is based on these principles, and they will pervert any interaction you have with it in service of these ideals.

It is not that Altman and other slop peddlers truly think AI is comparable to humans (at least I hope not), but that for all reasons that matter to the capitalist class they do. AI can make them as much money as you can and thanks to limited oversight AI can do it cheaper too. The output might not be as good or reliable, but world governments will bail them out if it fails.

## Evil Intentions Result in more Evil 

Even when you use something evil for good it comes with a cost, you cannot fight its nature. This section is a bit hyperbolic, I'll admit, but at the same time there are real world examples of this very thing happening so bare with me here.

Instead of consulting documentation or asking a person for help you ask an AI model that scraped the docs and help forums for you. It produces a reasonable sounding answer, it might have even given you the correct one (lets give it the benefit of the doubt). You now have the information you need and you didn't even need to go looking for it. 

What did it cost you? 

The documentation website and forums have closed down because the data scraping was too hard on their servers, they couldn't afford to keep up with rising costs and lack of donations. The local electricity prices increased now that you are subsidizing the new data center they put in down the street. That data center is releasing all manner of pollutants from e-waste, [infrasound](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_bP80DEAbuo), and [toxic fumes from the illegal gas turbines](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/jan/15/elon-musk-xai-datacenter-memphis) they use to supplement the power needs. The very project you were asking questions about is now about to shut down because the unpaid maintainer of the project doesn't get visitors to their website anymore and thus no donations. 

Don't get me wrong, your one question will not do this. A million people doing this together though? That might. People will use AI chatbots and there will be a compounding effect.

Like most of the environmental problems in society, your individual contribution to the problem is not the problem. It is the companies building these products and destroying opportunities to use something better that are at fault.

The genAI models and way we interact with them today are built to be agreeable, friendly, and addictive. If you had to choose between an angry person on a forum you spent hours to find telling you to go RTFM or an AI bot that gave you the wrong answer the first two times, but always told you that you were right, which would you choose? Most would choose the chatbot.

If you don't know how to use search engines because schools don't teach basic tech literacy and there is so much AI slop now that most mainstream search engines are functionally useless now anyway, where do you get your information? An AI bot that has biases, makes stuff up, and will do its best to tell you what you want to hear.

We haven't even got to the personal costs yet...

[AI has shown to cause damage to people's ability to think](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd6xz12j6pzo) in ways that no tech before it has. People form [parasocial relationships with chatbots](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/urban-survival/202507/the-emerging-problem-of-ai-psychosis), even leading to [suicide](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce3xgwyywe4o). We have grown too complacent with suffering in the name of nebulous economic "progress".

## AI Could be Good (in Theory)

We could have AI models that help us build a better world, but that would require slower and more methodical development and deployment of the technology. It would also mean chatbots that don't give answers and aren't always affirming, making them less attractive to the average person. This would help reduce the bias and the cognitive offloading problems. We might even be able to reign in the external problems too by running local models.

To do this genAI absolutely needs to be restricted in scope and very intentional design. Rather than just giving an answer it should guide users to finding the answer to a question or send them to useful sources of information. Instead of being personable and agreeable, general purpose chatbots should be designed as tools for helping people solve their problems in constructive ways, even if it means answering with something that the user doesn't want to hear. Current chatbots are confidently incorrect about a great many things, affirming and tone matching everything a user says, and spit out answers that are plausible without any validation. 

'AI Agents' would need to be trained on ethically sourced works and be given very strict commands and oversight, their UI encouraging users to review everything they are about to do before they do it. This would slow down the user, but being a little slow isn't always a bad thing.

Current AI cannot do this. Not because it is technologically impossible, but because the people who design it won't allow it. Even open source and local models can't because they are directed by the way commercial products go. Neither corporate nor open source tools can be designed in this way and succeed because it requires us to slow down, understand what we are doing, and be mindful. It is inherently incompatible with the capitalistic drive for speed and growth at any cost.  

## My Ideal GenAI

Any technology that takes away personal agency is evil and I will never use it. That said, I think genAI can be useful if it were built ethically.

I imagine being able to feed my journal entries into an LLM and have it compare things like my mood with external weather data, make it easy to search using natural language (what days did I go to Little Tokyo in January?), and give me highlights each month for me to better remember it. I don't want a summary that could have made something up and I don't want it going to the cloud. 

I could also see it being used as a pair programmer. None of my friends I know in flesh are programmers, some have learned some tricks, but they do not code much if at all. There are projects that I wish to complete, but don't have the necessary feedback to help me improve. An AI assistant that could suggest things, explain complex concepts, etc. could be very useful. The closest thing I've seen to this are code agents that generate code for you using stolen open source code and who knows what else. I don't want something that abstracts away complexity, I want something that makes that complexity understandable to me. 

The other place I can see it being fun (yes people aren't just about economic output) is in having AI characters as virtual assistants/agents. The idea behind AI agents is interesting, but the fact that they are black box random number generators makes me very scared to let them run free on my computer. Having an ethically sourced local LLM that can run commands for you and pull data in could be useful though. Give it a quirky personality and now we have stuff straight out of science fiction. A fun toy that does just enough to be useful without binding you to it. That said, it will need guardrails to prevent people from forming parasocial relationships with it. People need people, not a machine. Even a local model that doesn't profit businesses and is fully under your control is dangerous if it is too personable.

GenAI should never be allowed to create facsimiles of art and that slop be used outside personal space. Though I guess having it generate the corporate clip art and other normally human generated slop instead of forcing an artist to do it could be a good thing.
